What is esports ?

Esports short for electronic sports, is a form of competition using video games.[1] Esports often takes the form of organized, multiplayer video game competitions, particularly between professional players, played individually or as teams.[2] Multiplayer competitions were long a part of video game culture, but were largely between amateurs until the late 2000s, when the advent of online streaming media platforms, particularly YouTube and Twitch, enabled a surge in participation by professional gamers and spectators.[3][4] By the 2010s, esports was a major part of the video game industry, with many game developers designing for and funding for tournaments and other events. Esports first became popular in East Asia, particularly in China and South Korea (which first licensed professional players in 2000) but less so in Japan, whose broad anti-gambling laws prohibit professional gaming tournaments.[5][6] Esports are also popular in Europe and the Americas, which host regional and international events.

Tournament

Esports tournaments are competitive video gaming events where players or teams compete against each other in various video games for prizes and recognition. These tournaments can be organized for different game genres such as first-person shooters, real-time strategy, multiplayer online battle arena (MOBA), fighting games, and sports simulations. Some of the most popular esports tournaments and games include: League of Legends World Championship (Worlds) - Organized by Riot Games, this annual event is one of the biggest in the esports calendar, attracting millions of viewers worldwide. The International (Dota 2) - Hosted by Valve, The International is known for its massive prize pools, often crowdfunded by the game's community. CS Major Championships - These are some of the most prestigious events in the Counter-Strike: Global Offensive scene, organized by various hosts with Valve's support. Fortnite World Cup - Epic Games hosts this event, offering significant prize money and drawing a large, diverse audience. Overwatch League (OWL) - Blizzard Entertainment's professional esports league for Overwatch, featuring city-based teams from around the world. EVO Championship Series - A prominent fighting game tournament featuring titles like Street Fighter, Tekken, and Super Smash Bros. Call of Duty League (CDL) - An esports league for Call of Duty, featuring franchised teams from different cities. Rocket League Championship Series (RLCS) - Psyonix organizes this tournament for Rocket League, combining soccer with vehicular acrobatics. These events are typically live-streamed on platforms like Twitch, YouTube, and other streaming services, allowing fans from around the world to watch and engage with the competitions in real-time.
